# Educational Game Design Concept: "Math Kingdom" Strategy

This document summarizes the core design principles and mechanics for an addictive educational game focused on mathematics, kingdom-building, and progression.

## Core Game Loop
The game utilizes a dual-phase experience to maintain engagement and manage player fatigue:

1.  **The Sprint (High Intensity):**
    * Players solve fast-paced math problems to earn "Raw Materials" (stone, wood, gold).
    * **Dynamic Difficulty:** Problems adjust in real-time. If a player struggles, the game softens the difficulty to prevent frustration.
    * **Hint System:** Players can request step-by-step guidance.
        * Each step of the hint costs a small amount of in-game currency.
        * This encourages players to solve independently while providing a safety net.
    * **Flow State:** The speed and challenge scale to create a "flow" state, keeping the player focused and motivated.

2.  **The Strategy (Relaxing Break):**
    * Once materials are earned, players enter a building phase to construct their base or kingdom.
    * This phase features chill background music to contrast with the high-intensity math rounds.
    * **Construction Puzzles:** Uses a "Blueprint" system where players use materials to solve non-timed logic/geometric puzzles to complete structures.

## Reward & Progression System
* **Currency:** Points earned in the "Sprint" phase are used for both hints and building resources/upgrades.
* **Collectibles:** Players can earn/unlock pets and base decorations.
* **Legendary Items:** High-difficulty math challenges reward players with rare, "Legendary" status symbols, providing a goal for advanced learners.
* **Social Connectivity:**
    * **Friends-Only Trading:** Players can trade items and pets exclusively with friends, fostering a tight-knit community and a secure internal economy.
    * **Long-Term Goal:** Social interaction and visiting friends' kingdoms are unlocked as a long-term progression milestone.

## Design Philosophy
* **Balance:** High-risk, high-reward math sprints provide energy, while calm construction phases provide a necessary creative breather.
* **Motivation:** By allowing players to earn back points during action rounds, the game maintains a "one more try" addictive quality.
* **Choice:** Players must strategically decide between using points for immediate assistance (hints) or saving for long-term growth (building/collectibles).
